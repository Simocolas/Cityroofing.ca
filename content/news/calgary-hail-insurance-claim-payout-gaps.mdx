## The Shift from Replacement Cost to Actual Cash Value (ACV)

The biggest threat to your wallet right now is a transition from Replacement Cost Value (RCV) to Actual Cash Value (ACV). In the past, most [Calgary roof replacement](/services/roof-replacement) projects were covered by RCV policies, meaning the insurance company paid for the full cost of a new roof, regardless of how old your shingles were.

Now, many insurers are quietly switching homeowners to ACV schedules during policy renewals. Under ACV, the insurer subtracts depreciation based on the age of your roof. If your 15-year-old roof is hit by hail, they might only pay you 40% of what a new roof actually costs. 

| Policy Type | Coverage Detail | Estimated Out-of-Pocket Gap |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Replacement Cost (RCV)** | Full cost of new roof minus deductible | $1,000 - $2,500 |
| **Actual Cash Value (ACV)** | Depreciated value based on roof age | $8,000 - $12,000 |
| **Hail-Specific Deductible** | Higher flat-fee for hail events | $2,500 - $5,000 |
| **Code Upgrade Coverage** | Covers new bylaws/safety standards | $0 (If endorsed) |

Look — if you're on an ACV policy and a storm hits, you could be looking at a $10,000 gap between your insurance cheque and the actual cost of a quality roof.
