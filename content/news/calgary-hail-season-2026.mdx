Calgary experiences 15 to 20 significant hail events annually, making it one of Canada's highest hail-risk cities. After any hailstorm, homeowners should inspect gutters, vents, and shingles for damage within 24 hours. ## Why Calgary Is High-Risk for Hail

Calgary sits within what meteorologists call Hailstorm Alley, a corridor stretching from southern Alberta into Saskatchewan that experiences some of the most frequent and severe hail in North America. The collision of warm Pacific air with cold Arctic fronts creates ideal conditions for large hail formation, particularly between May and September.

## What to Do in the First 24 Hours After a Hailstorm

Document everything before making any repairs. Photograph your roof from ground level, check gutters for dents and granule buildup, inspect window screens and AC units, and note the date and approximate storm intensity. This documentation supports your insurance claim.

## Signs of Hail Damage on Your Roof

Asphalt shingles show hail damage as dark circular bruising or dents where granules have been knocked away. Metal components like vents, flashings, and gutters show more obvious denting. Flat roofs may show impact marks on membrane surfaces. When in doubt, a free professional inspection removes all guesswork.

## Working With Your Insurance Company

Contact your insurer within 48 hours of the storm. Most Alberta policies have a one-year window for hail claims, but earlier is always better. An adjuster will inspect the damage — having an independent contractor assessment beforehand gives you a strong reference point.
