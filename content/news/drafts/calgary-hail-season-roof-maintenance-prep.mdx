## Why May is the Critical Window for Calgary Roof Maintenance

In Calgary, May is the bridge between the spring melt and the summer storm season. It's the most important month for your roof. While many people wait until they see a leak to call a contractor, the most expensive damage often happens silently during the first major storm in June.

The transition from the freeze-thaw cycles of winter to the heavy rains of spring puts immense stress on your roofing system. We often see roofs that survived the winter but are "primed" for failure. For example, undetected ice damming from March can leave flashings loose or sealants cracked. When the first 20mm hail event hits in June, these weakened areas fail immediately.

May is your last chance to perform a [professional roof repair](https://calgarycityroofing.com/services/roof-repair) before the peak season begins. ### Identifying 'Shingle Fatigue' After a Calgary Winter

From our 15+ years working on Calgary homes, we've identified a phenomenon we call "shingle fatigue." Calgary’s unique climate—high elevation, intense UV exposure, and rapid temperature swings—causes asphalt shingles to expand and contract more aggressively than in other parts of Canada.

This "thermal cycling" has several effects on your roof:
- **Granule Loss:** The protective ceramic granules on your shingles loosen and wash into your gutters, leaving the underlying asphalt exposed to the sun.
- **Brittle Matting:** Over time, the shingles lose their flexibility. A shingle that can't flex is a shingle that will crack the moment a hailstone hits it.
- **Sealant Failure:** The adhesive strips that hold shingles down can weaken, making your roof more vulnerable to wind uplift during a thunderstorm.

When a shingle is fatigued, it might look fine from the ground. However, its structural integrity is compromised. A 10-year-old roof in Calgary often has the wear and tear of a 20-year-old roof in a more stable climate. This is why a spring inspection is so vital; we look for the subtle signs of fatigue that indicate your roof won't survive another season of "Hail Alley" weather.

## Is Your Roof Ready for 2026? Key Inspection Points

When we perform a spring assessment, we aren't just looking for missing shingles. We're looking for the "soft" indicators of future failure. If you're doing a preliminary check yourself, pay close attention to the soft metals on your roof—vents, chimney caps, and valley flashing. These often show "bruising" from smaller hail that shingles might hide.

If your roof is nearing the end of its life, or if you've had repeated claims, it may be time to consider a [full roof replacement](https://calgarycityroofing.com/services/roof-replacement). In Calgary, many homeowners are opting for Class 4 Impact Resistant shingles.

> **UL 2218 Class 4:** The industry standard for impact resistance. Shingles are tested by dropping steel balls from specific heights to ensure they can withstand significant force without cracking or losing structural integrity.

While Class 4 shingles aren't "hail-proof," they are significantly more resilient. They can often withstand the mid-sized hail that would typically shred a standard architectural shingle.

### Homeowner Checklist for May
- Clear all eavestroughs and downspouts to prevent water backup during heavy spring rains.
- Check for excessive granule loss in gutters, a sign of winter shingle degradation.
- Take date-stamped, high-resolution photos of all roof slopes and soft metals (vents, flashing).
- Inspect roof flashings around chimneys and skylights for gaps caused by winter ice damming.
- Schedule a professional inspection to identify 'bruised' shingles that are invisible from the ground.
- Review your insurance policy to confirm your current hail deductible and coverage limits.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What roof maintenance should I do before Calgary hail season starts in June?**
A: Focus on clearing gutters, inspecting for granule loss, and documenting the roof's condition with photos. A professional inspection is recommended to find winter damage, like sealant failure or brittle matting, that could lead to leaks during a heavy hail storm.

**Q: Does a spring roof inspection help with insurance claims in Calgary?**
A: Yes. It provides date-stamped proof of your roof's condition before the storm season. This helps prevent insurers from attributing new hail damage to old wear and tear, which is a common reason for claim denials in Alberta's high-risk "Hail Alley."
