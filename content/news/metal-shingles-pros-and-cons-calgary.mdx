### The "Pros" of Metal Shingles in Calgary

1.  **Impact Resistance that Actually Works:** Most metal shingles carry a Class 4 impact rating. In Calgary, where hail is a "when" not an "if," this is the gold standard. While asphalt shingles lose their protective granules under heavy hail, metal shingles maintain their structural integrity.
2.  **Weight and Structural Safety:** Believe it or not, metal is significantly lighter than asphalt. A metal roof puts less stress on your rafters and trusses. This is a massive advantage for older homes in neighbourhoods like Mount Royal or Capitol Hill that might not have been built for the weight of modern, heavy-duty laminate shingles.
3.  **Snow Shedding and Ice Dam Prevention:** Calgary’s freeze-thaw cycles are brutal on roofs. Metal warms up faster in the sun, helping to shed snow and preventing the massive ice build-ups that lead to leaks. 
4.  **Longevity:** You’re looking at a 50-year lifespan. If you’re in your "forever home," this is likely the last roof you’ll ever buy.

### The "Cons" to Consider

1.  **Upfront Investment:** There’s no sugar-coating it—the initial cost is high. You’re paying for premium materials and a much more technical installation process.
2.  **Installation Complexity:** You can’t just "nail down" metal shingles like you do with asphalt. They require specific flashing details, specialized underlayments, and precise venting. If the crew isn't experienced (which is why we never subcontract), you'll end up with leaks at the valleys and chimneys.
3.  **The "Dent" Factor:** While metal won't crack or leak after hail, very large stones can leave cosmetic dings. For some homeowners, this is a deal-breaker; for others, a few small dents are a fair trade-off for a roof that didn't fail.

## Is the Noise a Real Problem?

One of the biggest myths we hear is that metal roofs are noisy during rain. Honestly, if your home is built to modern Calgary standards with proper attic insulation and solid roof decking, you won't notice a difference. The "pinging" sound people associate with metal only happens on open-frame structures like sheds or carports. In a residential setting, the attic space acts as a massive sound buffer.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Will a metal roof make my house hotter in the summer?**
A: Actually, it’s the opposite. Metal shingles reflect solar radiant heat rather than absorbing it like dark asphalt. This can actually reduce your cooling costs during those hot Calgary July weeks.

**Q: Can you walk on a metal shingle roof?**
A: Yes, but you have to know where to step. Most manufacturers design them to be walkable, but our crews use specialized soft-soled shoes to make sure we don't scuff the finish or cause buckling.

**Q: Do metal shingles rust in Calgary's climate?**
A: No. Modern metal shingles from suppliers like Vicwest or Decra use high-tech coatings (like Galvalume or specialized polymers) that are specifically designed to resist corrosion and UV fading for decades.

**Q: Is a metal roof more likely to be hit by lightning?**
A: This is a common myth. Lightning is attracted to the highest point, not the material. If your house is hit, metal is actually safer because it's non-combustible and will help dissipate the electrical charge.
