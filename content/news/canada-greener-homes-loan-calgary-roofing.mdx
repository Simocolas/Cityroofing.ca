## Stopping the Chinook Cycle: Insulation and Ice Dam Prevention

Calgary's freeze-thaw cycles between October and April are a nightmare for roofs. When your attic isn't insulated properly, heat escapes from your house and warms the underside of your roof deck. This melts the snow sitting on top. That water then runs down to the cold eaves and freezes, creating a block of ice—an ice dam.

Upgrading to R-60 insulation standards is your first line of defense. It keeps the heat in your house and keeps the roof deck cold. From what we see on roofs across Calgary's older NW neighbourhoods like Varsity or SW spots like Lakeview, most ice dam problems trace back to one thing: attic ventilation that was never updated when the insulation was.

These 1960s and 70s homes were built for a different era of energy costs. Today, those original insulation levels are nowhere near the current Alberta Building Code requirements. By using the 0% interest loan, you can bring an older home up to modern standards without the immediate sting of a massive bill. If you've noticed giant icicles or water stains on your ceiling after a warm spell, you likely need a [professional roof repair](/services/roof-repair) and an insulation top-up.

## The Application Process: Precision Estimates for Loan Approval

The Canada Greener Homes Loan isn't a "blank check" program. To get approved, you need an NRCan-licensed energy auditor to visit your home before and after the work. They'll perform a blower door test and look at your current insulation levels.

This is where the paperwork gets technical. Federal loan administrators require line-item precision. They won't approve a vague "fix the roof" estimate. They need to see the specific R-value of the insulation being added and how the ventilation is being improved.
